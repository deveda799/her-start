# CodeBanana Guide for Editors & Content Writers

> Editors never have enough time — brainstorming topics, writing first drafts, proofreading meticulously, adapting for different platforms. CodeBanana compresses the "zero to first draft" phase, freeing you to focus on what truly showcases editorial skill: polishing prose, controlling tone, and making content judgments.

---

## Scenario 1: First Draft of 2,000 Words in 15 Minutes

### When You'd Use This

- You have a topic but no time to write from scratch — need a first draft as a starting point
- You have raw materials and data that need to be quickly assembled into a complete article
- Deadline is approaching — need to get something out first, then refine

### How to Do It

Tell the Agent your topic and requirements:

> "Write an article about 'How SMBs Can Use AI to Cut Costs and Boost Efficiency' — target 2,000 words, casual and engaging tone"

The Agent delivers more than just body text:
- 1 primary headline + 2 alternates
- 100-word summary
- Complete article with clear sections and subheadings
- Auto-adapted style: short paragraphs, key points bolded, conversational tone

Something feels off?

> "Make the tone a bit more formal, add data points to section two"

30 seconds to adjust. Polish the draft, inject your own perspective and sources — way faster than starting from scratch.

### Result

Previously: writing 2,000 words from scratch took at least half a day. Now: first draft in 15 minutes. Use the saved time for interviews, fine-tuning details, and aligning with editorial direction.

## Scenario 2: Full-Text Proofread in 3 Minutes

### When You'd Use This

- Article is done, needs a final check before publishing
- Worried about typos and grammar issues after reading it three times already
- Contains data citations and facts that need accuracy verification

### How to Do It

Hand the full text to the Agent:

> "Proofread this 3,000-word product launch announcement"

The Agent returns a proofing report in 3 minutes:

- **Typos**: "the product has already been lauched" → "launched"
- **Grammar**: "This not only improves efficiency, but also it reduced costs" → tense inconsistency
- **Fact check**: The market size figure cited should be verified against the latest source
- **Formatting**: Inconsistent number formats, missing transition sentences between paragraphs

> "Apply all your suggestions"

The Agent outputs the corrected full text directly.

### Result

Even after reading three times, you might miss typos — the human brain naturally auto-corrects. The Agent doesn't. It checks word by word — typos, grammar, factual citations, all in one pass.

## Scenario 3: One Article, Multiple Platforms in 5 Minutes

### When You'd Use This

- One article needs to go out on your blog, Medium, LinkedIn, and Twitter/X simultaneously
- Each platform has a different style, and manually adapting means four rounds of editing
- The boss says "distribute this across all channels"

### How to Do It

> "Take this 3,000-word blog post and adapt it for Medium, LinkedIn, and a Twitter/X thread"

The Agent delivers three versions in 5 minutes, each precisely adapted:

**Medium version (1,500 words):** Added depth and data citations, "bottom line up front" opening, discussion-prompting conclusion

**LinkedIn version (500 words):** Professional but approachable tone, bullet points, key takeaway callouts, engagement question at the end

**Twitter/X thread (8 tweets):** Hook in the first tweet, one key point per tweet, thread summary at the end with a CTA

Each version understands the platform's "unwritten rules" — Medium readers want depth and data, LinkedIn readers want professional insights, Twitter/X needs punchy hooks that stop the scroll.

### Result

Previously: adapting one article for 4 platforms took most of a day. Now: all versions in 5 minutes, each precisely fitted to its platform. You just do a quick review to confirm the tone.

## Tips for Editors

1. **The first draft is a starting point, not the finish line**: The Agent's draft will be structurally complete but may lack your personal voice and unique insights. Best workflow: Agent builds the framework → you add the soul.

2. **Automate topic inspiration with Heartbeat**: In Cron Job, enable Heartbeat and add "search for industry hot topics and trending themes, compile 5 topic directions" to HEARTBEAT.md — set the interval for every morning. By the time you arrive at the office, today's writing direction is already waiting. If you only need weekday updates, Scheduled Tasks with Weekly type offers more precision.

3. **Push editorial reviews to IM**: In Agent Config → Channel, connect to Slack, Feishu, or DingTalk. After finalizing a draft, have the Agent push the article summary and link to the review channel — the editor-in-chief responds directly in the group, no tool-switching needed.

4. **Produce all platform versions at once**: After finishing one article, build the habit of having the Agent generate all platform adaptations at once. It takes 5 minutes and doubles your content reach.

5. **Lock in your writing standards with Basic Info**: In Soul, write "you are a senior content editor; writing style is concise and punchy, favors short sentences and metaphors, avoids corporate jargon and buzzwords." In User, specify your target audience and platform preferences — "primarily producing blog and LinkedIn content, targeting SMB decision-makers." The Agent's first drafts will automatically align with your standards from the start.
