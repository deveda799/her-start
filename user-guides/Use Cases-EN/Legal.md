# CodeBanana Guide for Legal Teams

> A huge portion of legal work is spent on "reading text" — drafting contracts clause by clause, reviewing contracts clause by clause, comparing versions word by word. What these tasks have in common: they require intense focus, but the pattern is repeatable. CodeBanana can't make legal judgments for you, but it can dramatically accelerate the text processing, so you can focus your expertise where it truly matters.

---

## Scenario 1: Draft a Contract in 15 Minutes

### When You'd Use This

- A business team says "we need a contract" and you're starting from scratch
- You have a template but need to customize it for a specific client and scenario
- Need to quickly produce a framework, then refine later

### How to Do It

Give the Agent the business context:

> "Draft a SaaS service agreement. We're the service provider, the client is a bank, annual fee $50K, 1-year term, SLA 99.9%"

The Agent delivers a complete contract draft in 15 minutes: definitions and interpretations, service scope, fees and payment, SLA commitments and remedies, data security and confidentiality… every section covered. It automatically considers industry specifics (e.g., adding financial compliance clauses and data processing agreements for a banking client).

Need adjustments?

> "Add a non-compete clause, change SLA penalty to 10% of monthly fee"

30 seconds to update. Do your professional review and refinements on this draft — far more efficient than writing from scratch.

### Result

Previously: drafting a contract — finding templates, referencing precedents, writing clause by clause — at least 2–3 days. Now: a complete first draft in 15 minutes. Use the saved time for legal reasoning and risk assessment.

## Scenario 2: Full Contract Risk Review in 10 Minutes

### When You'd Use This

- The other party sent their contract and you need to quickly identify unfavorable terms
- Doing a comprehensive risk assessment before signing
- Business is pushing "can we sign today?" and you need a fast verdict

### How to Do It

Contract content is typically sensitive — use **Private Agent**. Only you can see the conversation; it won't appear in the team's Team Agent.

Send the contract file to the Agent:

> "Review this procurement contract the client sent over"

The Agent produces a risk review report in 10 minutes, categorized by severity:

**🔴 High Risk (must change):**
- Unlimited joint liability clause — no cap on damages
- Unilateral termination without compensation — other party can terminate at will

**🟡 Medium Risk (recommend changing):**
- Ambiguous IP ownership — shared rights invite disputes
- Harsh payment terms
- Undefined acceptance criteria

Each risk point is tagged with: source location in the contract, risk analysis, and suggested modification.

> "Mark all the suggested changes directly in the contract"

The Agent inserts annotations at the relevant positions and outputs a marked-up version — send it directly to the other side for negotiation.

### Result

Previously: reviewing a contract line by line took 1–2 hours. Now: a tiered risk report in 10 minutes — high, medium, low at a glance. You can focus your attention on the legal judgment for high-risk clauses instead of spending it on word-by-word reading.

## Scenario 3: Compare Two Contract Versions in 3 Minutes

### When You'd Use This

- The other party returned a revised version and you need to confirm every change
- After several negotiation rounds, you need to verify your suggested edits were accepted
- Contract was edited by multiple people — needs a full comparison with the original

### How to Do It

Send both versions to the Agent:

> "The other party sent back their revised version — compare it with our last version"

The Agent produces a complete comparison report in 3 minutes:

- **Significant changes**: Payment period changed from 15 to 45 days, liability cap reduced, termination rights changed to "Party A only"
- **Minor changes**: New exclusion clause added, acceptance process modified
- **Our suggestions adopted**: IP ownership, confidentiality period, dispute resolution — 3 items modified per our request

Each difference is tagged with side-by-side text and impact analysis.

> "Highlight disagreements in red, agreements in green, export as Word"

The Agent applies the color coding and outputs an annotated Word document. Take this comparison into negotiations — every change is crystal clear.

### Result

Previously: comparing two contract versions word by word took 1 hour, with constant worry about missing subtle changes. Now: a complete comparison in 3 minutes, with zero missed differences.

## Tips for Legal Teams

1. **The Agent produces drafts, not final documents**: Contracts involve legal risk — the Agent accelerates text processing, but the final legal judgment must be yours. Think of it as an extremely fast assistant, not a lawyer.

2. **Use Private Agent for sensitive contracts**: Undisclosed deal terms, non-compete agreements, M&A documents — handle these in Private Agent. Only you can see the content; the rest of the team cannot.

3. **Build your own template library**: After every drafting or review session, save the final version in the project. Next time you face a similar scenario, tell the Agent "reference the SaaS agreement template from last time" — much more efficient.

4. **Make version comparison a habit**: After every negotiation round, run a comparison with the Agent to ensure no changes were missed. This 3-minute investment prevents many downstream disputes.

5. **Set legal boundaries in Basic Info**: In Soul, explicitly write "you are a legal assistant; responsible only for text processing and risk flagging, not final legal judgments. For ambiguous clauses, label as 'requires human review' rather than making a determination." In User, write "legal role, familiar with contract law and corporate law, output format follows legal document standards." This keeps the Agent efficient without overstepping.

6. **Use Scheduled Tasks for contract expiry reminders**: In Cron Job, create "once" tasks for important contracts — e.g., "remind me 30 days before contract expiry to initiate renewal." Multiple contracts? Create multiple tasks. The Agent reminds you on schedule — never miss a renewal window.
